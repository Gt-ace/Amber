# Amber — Brand Brief (final)

**One line**
Amber is a self-hostable personal canvas — your software, your server, your files.

**Why it exists**
The web pushes everyone onto rented platforms that own your content. Amber is the
opposite bet: markdown on your own disk, hand-editable config, AGPL, no lock-in.
You can leave anytime because the files were always yours.

**The name**
Amber = fossilized resin. It *preserves* — captures something and keeps it intact,
warm and golden, indefinitely. Your content, preserved, yours. (Built-in color
anchor: warm amber / honey / gold.)

**Audience — optimize for non-technical creators first**
Lead with approachability. The person who wants a beautiful personal site or
link-in-bio and never wants to see a terminal. The authoring layer (v0.5) is the
front door. The technical self-hoster core still has to *trust* it — but the brand
greets the newcomer first.

**Positioning**
Not a SaaS, not a startup, not enterprise. Sits with Bear, Ghost, Obsidian, the
small-web movement — calm, durable, personal-scale software that respects the user.
Quietly replaces Linktree and Carrd.

**Personality — calm & sovereign, worn warmly**
Calm · Sovereign · Warm · Honest · Durable. Understated and premium, but soft and
human — never cold, never intimidating, never "disruptive." The reassurance of
owning something outright, offered with an open hand.

**Voice & tone**
Plain-spoken, precise, confident without hype. Treats the reader as a capable adult
— but explains, never gatekeeps. Warmer and gentler in the product UI than in the
developer docs.

**How it should feel**
Like a well-made object you own outright — a good notebook, a paid-for tool that
doesn't phone home. Permanent, in a web that feels rented. Calm enough to trust,
warm enough to enjoy.

**Visual direction cues** (feeding palette → logo → type)
- Warm amber / honey / gold anchor against a calm neutral — warm off-white for
  light, soft deep charcoal (not pure black) for dark.
- The mark is a **faceted, translucent amber gemstone that glows from within** —
  dimensional and jewel-like, in the spirit of the Obsidian app logo but in warm
  amber. Amber *is* a gem; the glow is the magic and is material-honest, not a
  trend gradient. Premium and calm, never glossy or neon. Simple silhouette so it
  reads at favicon size. Not a literal resin-drip or bug-in-amber.
- In product UI / typography, stay restrained: generous whitespace, soft geometry.
  The jewel is the hero; everything around it is quiet. "Less, but yours."

**What to avoid**
Tech-startup gradients, neon, glassmorphism. Hacker/terminal coldness. Anything
implying a cloud service that owns your data. Over-design — the pitch is *less*.
